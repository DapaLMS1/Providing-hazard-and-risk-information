Senior Dental Assistant / Supervisor WHS Responsibilities

Explore the key WHS roles and duties of senior dental assistants.

Direct WHS Implementation & Oversight

As a Senior Dental Assistant or Supervisor, you are crucial in ensuring WHS policies are put into practice daily. This involves:

  • Leading by Example: Consistently demonstrating best WHS practices for others to follow, setting the standard for safety.
  • Daily Monitoring: Regularly checking that WHS policies and procedures (e.g., infection control, sharps disposal, equipment cleaning) are being strictly followed by all dental assistants.
  • Troubleshooting: Addressing minor WHS issues or non-compliance immediately and effectively to prevent escalation.

Training & Mentorship

A key aspect of your role is to support the development of your team in WHS. This includes:

  • On-the-Job Training: Providing practical, hands-on WHS training to new or less experienced dental assistants, reinforcing what was covered in formal inductions.
  • Guidance: Acting as a primary point of contact for dental assistants with WHS questions or concerns, offering clear and helpful advice.
  • Skill Development: Helping to ensure dental assistants are competent in using new equipment safely or adapting to new procedures, ensuring continuous improvement in WHS practices.

Hazard & Incident Management

You play a vital role in the early detection and management of safety issues:

  • First Point of Contact for Reporting: Often the initial person dental assistants report hazards, near misses, or minor incidents to.
  • Initial Assessment: Assisting in the initial assessment of hazards or incidents before escalating to the practice manager for further action.
  • Corrective Actions: Implementing immediate corrective actions for minor WHS breaches or identified hazards, as directed by management, to mitigate risks promptly.

Communication & Feedback

Effective communication is essential for a strong WHS culture. Your responsibilities include:

  • Two-Way Communication: Facilitating open communication between dental assistants and management regarding WHS concerns, suggestions, or feedback.
  • WHS Meeting Input: Providing practical input from the dental assistant's perspective during WHS discussions at staff meetings, ensuring relevant insights are shared.
  • Documentation Support: Assisting the practice manager with WHS documentation related to incidents, training records, or equipment checks, contributing to accurate record-keeping.

Resource Management (Daily Level)

You help manage the practical aspects of WHS resources on a daily basis:

  • PPE Oversight: Ensuring adequate supply and correct use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) by dental assistants, promoting compliance.
  • Supply Management: Overseeing the safe storage and handling of clinical supplies and chemicals within their designated areas, maintaining a secure environment.
