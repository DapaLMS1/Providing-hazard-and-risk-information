Senior Dental Assistant / Supervisor WHS Responsibilities

Explore the key WHS roles and duties of senior dental assistants.

Direct WHS Implementation & Oversight

As a Senior Dental Assistant or Supervisor, you are crucial in ensuring WHS policies are put into practice daily. This involves:

  • Leading by Example: Demonstrating best WHS practices.
  • Daily Monitoring: Ensuring policies are followed.
  • Troubleshooting: Addressing minor WHS issues.

Training & Mentorship

Support your team by:

  • On-the-Job Training: Practical, hands-on support.
  • Guidance: Clear answers to WHS questions.
  • Skill Development: Ensuring safe, up-to-date practices.

Hazard & Incident Management

Your responsibilities include:

  • First Point of Contact: For hazard reports.
  • Initial Assessment: Review issues before escalation.
  • Corrective Actions: Apply fixes immediately when safe.

Communication & Feedback

Maintain open communication:

  • Two-Way Dialogue: Encourage questions and input.
  • WHS Meetings: Provide valuable perspectives.
  • Documentation: Assist in maintaining accurate records.

Resource Management (Daily Level)

Ensure all tools and supplies support WHS:

  • PPE Oversight: Confirm availability and correct use.
  • Safe Storage: Manage chemicals and tools correctly.
